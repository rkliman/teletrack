About Us

We are a group of Electrical and Computer engineers at Georgia Insitute of Techonology trying to solve an on-going problems in our society. With Covid – 19 affecting the whole world, people are forced to communicate virtually, which can be somewhat limited. Body languages and gestures play a huge role in conversation and some will even argue that body languages are more important than verbal communication. To alleviate this problem, we came up with the idea of a web-controlled robot that does certain gestures depending on the user's control . 


Telepresence Robot

Telepresence robots are robots specialized for attending in-person meetings virtually. They are usually small, mobile devices, capable of sending and receiving audio and visual data as though the users are in a teleconference meeting, with the difference that the user is able to control the movement of the robot. They can be very expensive. A telepresence robot made by the company Double Robotics costs at least four thousand dollars. Most robots of this kind are generalized, and roll around on the floor, keeping the camera and screen at eye level using gyroscopes to steady the base of the robot. This team’s robot will be more specialized and move only along a wall-mounted track. While a wall-mounted track will restrict the robot’s movement, it will also keep the robot steady without a gyroscope and allow for several cost saving measures. Other robot’s also restrict the user’s control of the robot to its movement. This robot has a moveable arm equipped with a laser pointer that the user can use to gesture and indicate specific things in the robot’s environment.
